Notify a public sector pension provider

We've found 1 public sector pension that matches the National Insurance number of the person who's died: {{ data['ni-number'] | formatNiNumber }}.

That pension is provided by: Civil Service Pension.

This does not include State Pension. The State Pension is not a public sector pension. It’s a regular payment from the Department of Work and Pensions (DWP) based on the person's National Insurance record. Public sector pensions are for people who worked in the public sector, for example, in the NHS, local government or civil service.

When you notify the pension provider about the death they will:

  • stop any regular payments
  • identify any people or organisations who were named to get the pension after the death
  • close the account

We’ve not checked all public sector pension providers.

Which public sector pension providers we've checked

We've checked for any public sector pensions that were provided by:

  • Civil Service Pension Scheme
  • NHS Pensions for NHS staff in England and Wales
  • Scottish Public Pension Agency schemes for NHS staff, teachers, police and firefighters in Scotland
  • Pension Protection Fund and Financial Assistance Scheme
  • Local Government Pension Schemes (LGPS)

If the person who died had a public sector pension from a different provider, contact the pension provider directly to notify them of the death.
