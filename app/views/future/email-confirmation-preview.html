What you can do next

If the person who died was claiming benefits with, or that were linked to, someone else

When you notify a benefit provider (for example, DWP, HMRC or local council), about the death, any payments to the person who died will stop.

If the person who died was claiming benefits with, or getting benefits that were linked to, someone else (for example as their partner, parent or carer) the surviving person must notify their benefit provider directly.

This is because the claim may need to change, or they may need to make a new claim to continue getting support.

Check how benefits are affected if your:

  • child has died: https://www.gov.uk/after-a-death/if-a-child-or-baby-dies
  • partner has died: https://www.gov.uk/death-spouse-benefits-tax-pension/benefits

Cut off the corner of the driving licence

You do not need to return the driving licence. Cutting the corner means the driving licence cannot be used.

Tell DVLA if you sell, transfer or keep the vehicle

When DVLA (Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency) remove the person who died as the registered keeper, the vehicle tax will no longer be valid.

If you sell, transfer or keep the vehicle you must tell the DVLA: https://www.gov.uk/sold-bought-vehicle

If you keep the vehicle you must tax it in your name.

Cut off the top right corner of the passport

You do not need to return the passport. Cutting the corner means the passport cannot be used.

If they had a public sector pension

If the person who died had a public sector pension, contact the pension provider directly to close the account and stop any regular payments.

Bereavement benefits and support

Check if you’re eligible for extra money for living costs

If your partner has died, and you were under State Pension age when they died, you can check if you can get Bereavement Support Payment at: https://www.gov.uk/bereavement-support-payment

Their partner can check if they’re eligible for extra money for living costs

Based on your answers, the partner of the person who died may be able to get extra money to help with their living costs if they’re on a low income. They can check if they can get Pension Credit at: https://www.gov.uk/pension-credit

The partner can check if they can get Bereavement Support Payment

If the partner of the person who died was under State Pension age when they died, they can check if they can get Bereavement Support Payment at: https://www.gov.uk/bereavement-support-payment

Other support you may be able to get

If you get certain benefits, you can check if you can get help with funeral costs at: https://www.gov.uk/funeral-payments

You can go to https://www.gov.uk/when-someone-dies to:

  • check if you’re eligible for other bereavement benefits
  • find bereavement support and services
