When you notify a benefit provider

When you notify a benefit provider (for example, DWP, HMRC or local council) about the death, payments to the person who died will stop.

If the person who died was claiming benefits with, or that were linked to, someone else (for example as their partner, parent or carer) the surviving person must notify their benefit provider directly. They must do this even if you've used Tell Us Once.

This is because the claim may need to change, or they may need to make a new claim to continue getting support.

Tax, benefits, entitlements including State Pension

If they received State Pension or other DWP benefits such as Attendance Allowance.
What is a DWP benefit or entitlement?

If you're not sure if they receive a DWP benefit, select yes. DWP can then check and update their records.

The most common DWP benefits and entitlements include:

  • Attendance Allowance (AA)
  • Disability Living Allowance (DLA)
  • Emplyoment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Jobseeker's Allowance
  • Pension Credit
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • State Pension
  • Universal Credit
  • Winter Fuel Payment

Other DWP benefits and entitlements include:

  • Bereavement Support Payment (BSP)
  • Incapacity Benefit
  • Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit
  • Maternity Allowance
  • Widowed Parent's Allowance (WPA)
For personal and individual tax records such as Pensions, Pay As You Earn (PAYE), Self Assessment or Self Employment.
If they received any HMRC benefits and/or credits when they died.

When DVLA is notified of the death, the vehicle tax will no longer be valid. If the vehicle is going to be driven, or is parked on a public road, it will need to be re-taxed. If there are more than five vehicles you will need to contact DVLA directly.
