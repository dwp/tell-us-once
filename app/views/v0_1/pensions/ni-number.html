Their National Insurance number

If you know the National Insurance number of the person who died:

  • some of the organisations and departments that you choose to notify, such as DWP and HMRC, will use it to find their records quicker
  • we will use it to find any public sector pensions they had

The National Insurance number is on their National Insurance card, payslip or P60. If they got state pension or benefits, it may be on a benefit letter or in the transaction description on a bank statement.

Which public sector pensions we will check

A public sector pension is a workplace pension for public sector employees. For example, civil servants, NHS and local government workers.

It's different to the State Pension, which is a regular payment from the government based on someone's National Insurance record.

We will check for pensions that were provided by:

  • Civil Service Pension Scheme
  • NHS Pensions for NHS staff in England and Wales
  • Scottish Public Pension Agency schemes for NHS staff, teachers, police and firefighters in Scotland
  • Pension Protection Fund and Financial Assistance Scheme
  • Local Government Pension Schemes (LGPS)

If the person who died had a pension from a different provider, contact the pension provider directly to notify them of the death.
