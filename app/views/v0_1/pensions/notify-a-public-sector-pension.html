Which public sector pensions we've checked

A public sector pension is a workplace pension for public sector employees. For example, civil servants, NHS and local government workers.

It's different to the State Pension, which is a regular payment from the government based on someone's National Insurance record.

We've checked for any pensions that were provided by:

  • Civil Service Pension Scheme
  • NHS Pensions for NHS staff in England and Wales
  • Scottish Public Pension Agency schemes for NHS staff, teachers, police and firefighters in Scotland
  • Pension Protection Fund and Financial Assistance Scheme
  • Local Government Pension Schemes (LGPS)

If the person who died had a pension from a different pension provider, contact them directly to notify them of the death.
