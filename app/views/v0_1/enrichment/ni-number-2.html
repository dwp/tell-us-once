Their National Insurance number

If you know the National Insurance number of the person who died:

  • it can help some of the organisations and services that you choose to notify, such as DWP and HMRC, to find their records quicker
  • if they worked in the public sector, for example, in the NHS, local government or civil service, we will use it to find pensions they may have had (known as ‘public sector pensions’)

The National Insurance number is on their National Insurance card, payslip or P60. If they got benefits, it may be on a benefit letter or bank statement.

We do not check all public sector pension providers.

Which public sector pension providers we will check

We will check for public sector pensions that were provided by:

  • Civil Service Pension Scheme
  • NHS Pensions for NHS staff in England and Wales
  • Scottish Public Pension Agency schemes for NHS staff, teachers, police and firefighters in Scotland
  • Pension Protection Fund and Financial Assistance Scheme
  • Local Government Pension Schemes (LGPS)

If the person who died had a public sectory pension from a different provider, contact the pension provider directly to notify them of the death.

If the person who died had a State Pension

The State Pension is not a public sector pension. It’s a regular payment from the Department of Work and Pensions (DWP) based on the person's National Insurance record.

You can notify DWP later in Tell Us Once.
